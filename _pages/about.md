Hi there! I am Haofei Xu, a Ph.D. student in Computational and Data Science at Washington University in St. Louis, where I began my doctoral studies in August 2025. I am passionate about leveraging computational methods to understand complex social and political phenomena, with a focus on natural language processing, machine learning, and large-scale data analysis.

My research centers on applying advanced computational techniques to analyze political discourse and social behavior. I have extensive experience in developing scalable data pipelines, implementing state-of-the-art NLP models, and conducting large-scale text analysis. My work spans from sentiment analysis of political communications to topic modeling of public discourse in local governance.

I bring a unique interdisciplinary perspective to my research, combining my background in social psychology with expertise in computer science, statistics, and data engineering. This diverse foundation allows me to bridge the gap between technical innovation and social science applications, developing computational solutions that provide meaningful insights into human behavior and democratic processes.

Currently, I work as a Data Engineer at J&K Scientific LLC, where I design and implement automated data extraction and processing systems using cloud technologies. My professional experience complements my academic research, providing me with practical skills in building robust, scalable data infrastructure.

Recent Research Highlights
======
My current research focuses on computational analysis of political discourse and democratic participation. Key projects include:

- **Large-Scale Political Discourse Analysis**: Leading a project analyzing public comments from 500+ Michigan municipalities, implementing scalable ETL pipelines on AWS and advanced NLP techniques including BERTopic for topic modeling.

- **Election Sentiment Analysis**: Developed sentiment analysis models for 2024 US Election tweets using transformer models (DistilBERT), achieving 98.54% accuracy on 1M+ tweet dataset.

- **Accessibility Research**: Conducted comparative analysis of web accessibility standards between US and Chinese universities, combining computational analysis with field studies.
