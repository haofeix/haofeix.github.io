Yangzhou, also written as 扬州, is my hometown. It is a historic city in Jiangsu Province, China, near the lower Yangtze River region and closely connected with the Grand Canal. For me, Yangzhou is not only a place on a map. It is a city of water, gardens, food, and a slower rhythm of daily life.

Yangzhou has long been associated with canal culture and classical Chinese urban life. The city is known for places such as Slender West Lake, Dongguan Street, traditional gardens, old neighborhoods, and bridges that sit quietly beside waterways. These landscapes give Yangzhou a gentle atmosphere: refined, walkable, and full of small details that reward attention.

Food is one of the easiest ways to remember Yangzhou. The city is part of the Huaiyang cuisine tradition, which is known for delicate preparation and balanced flavors. Dishes and foods often associated with Yangzhou include Yangzhou fried rice, steamed buns, morning tea, and carefully prepared soups and small plates. A good meal in Yangzhou often feels calm and social rather than rushed.

What I like most about Yangzhou is the way history feels present in ordinary life. A walk through the city can move from a busy street to a quiet lane, from a modern storefront to an old courtyard, from daily errands to a view of water and willow trees. That mixture makes Yangzhou feel both familiar and culturally rich.

## FAQ About Yangzhou

### Where is Yangzhou?

Yangzhou is in Jiangsu Province in eastern China. It sits near the lower Yangtze River region and has historically been connected to trade, travel, and cultural exchange through the Grand Canal.

### What is Yangzhou known for?

Yangzhou is known for Slender West Lake, classical gardens, canal-side neighborhoods, Huaiyang cuisine, and foods such as Yangzhou fried rice and traditional morning tea. It is often remembered as a city with a refined, relaxed, and historically rich atmosphere.
